John F. Kenendy International Airport

JFK airport is the largest aiport in the NYC metropolitan area. Built in 1963, JFK is the fifth busiest aiport in the US and the 22nd largest in the world.

Location

Located in southeast queens, JFK is about 45 minutes away from midtown manhattan by car. During rush hours it can take as long as 1.5 hours. However you can reach manhattan by either the subway or the Long Island Railroad. JFK is far for people from jersey and other west of the hudson but is much more ideal for people who live in westchester, manhattan, queens, brooklyn, the bronx and the western counties of long island.

Layout

JFK currently has 6 terminals, 1,2,4,5,7, and 8. Each terminal varies in size some containing as little as 10 gates and other having upwards of 48. Terminal 1 is a primary international terminal with airlines including Japan Airlines and Air France. It is relatively small and each gate is within walking distance. Terminal 2 is a delta domestic terminal. The terminal is small which means security is quickre but the terminal is very old and decrepit. Terminal 4 is the main international terminal at JFK and has delta's international flights, and other airlienas inlcluding SWISS and Virgin Atlantic. The terminal is large and modern, but the gates are very spread out so prepare to do some walking! Terminal 5 is jetblue's terminal that also plays host to aer lingus and Hawaiian. It's one of JFK's biggest and newest terminals and each gate is very close to the center of the terminal. Checking in here can be a pain, becuase of the large amount of passangers and the small amount of kiosks. Terminal 7 is British Airways' main terminal and also hosts airlinees such as cathay pacific. The terminal is one of JFK's oldest and smallest. Check and security is relaitvely painless but there is not much to look foward to after that. Terminal 8 is American airlines main terminal and other one-world allinace members such as qatar also are at terminal 8. It is modern, but the terminal is spread out and unserground walkway to most gates means you will be wlaking a lot.

Newark airport otherwiase known as EWR, is located in Newark, New Jersey. It is the buiset airport in the state of new jersey, the 14 busiset in the nation and the 43rd buisest in the world.

Location

Located in northeast new jersey, EWR is about 50 minutes to an hour outside of midtown manhattan by car without traffic. Besides the regional amtrak there is no train from EWR to manhattan. EWR is not an ideal location for people travelling from queens, brooklyn, westchester county, fairfield county or long island. It is ideal for those living in staten island or jersey.

Layout

EWR currently has 3 terminals, A, B, and C. Each tertminal is similar in layout each having three concrouses. They are equidistant from each other. Terminal A is a domestic terminal which houses jetblue, alaska, united express, air canada, southwest and domestic american airlines. Terminal B is the international terminal with arilines such as Delta, British airways, EL AL, Swiss and Air China. Terminal C is united's main terminal and the largest of the three. Each of the terminals is similar is style and layout and the exteriors look almost idetical. There is a tram that connects all of the terminals, so going from one to another is quite trivial.

Layout

Lagurdia currently has four terminals, A, B, C, D. Terminal A is the Alaska and Jetblue terminal. Terminal A is small (abput 6 gates) and old and lacks good food options but it is efficent, it takes a short time to get to the gate from check in, and it good for frequent flyers, however it is far from the other terminals. Terminal B is the largest terminal at lagurdia having over 30 gates. It is old but has undergone some recent renovations which has made the terminal a little bit more attractive. The airlines at terminal B are Air Canada, American Airlines, Southwest, and United airlines. Terminal C is the second biggest terminal and has two airlines, Delta and Spirit. Terminal C is older and will go through future renovations. Terminal D is the 3rd biggest terminal with about ten gates. Westjet, Spirit, Frontier, and Delta are the airlines at terminal D.
